## Welcome

The **Climate Adaptation & Vector Biology Lab** The Climate Adaptation Laboratory investigates how climate‑associated environmental stressors, particularly rising temperatures, disrupt biological systems across levels of organization from neurochemistry and behavior to fitness and population resilience. The lab adopts an integrative and comparative framework to understand how climate stress shapes human‑relevant neural processes and vector fitness and resistance, with implications for public health and disease dynamics.
Using Drosophila melanogaster as a powerful translational model for human neurochemistry and Anopheles mosquitoes as a disease‑relevant vector system, the laboratory combines biochemical analysis, stress physiology, and systems‑level approaches. By linking molecular and neurochemical responses to functional outcomes, the lab aims to identify early biomarkers, mechanistic bottlenecks, and adaptive limits imposed by climate change.

### Research Focus
- Thermal tolerance and stress physiology
- Fitness trade‑offs under heat stress
- Molecular and biochemical mechanisms
- Climate change and malaria transmission
